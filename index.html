History of Formula 1

Formula 1 (F1) began in 1950 with the first official World Championship race at Silverstone. Early dominance came from Alfa Romeo and Juan Manuel Fangio in the 1950s. The 1960s introduced mid-engine cars and British teams like Lotus and McLaren. The turbo era of the 1980s saw intense rivalries, notably Senna vs. Prost, and F1's commercial boom. Michael Schumacher and Ferrari dominated the early 2000s, followed by increased focus on aerodynamics and hybrid engines. Today, F1 is a global spectacle with cutting-edge technology, safety advancements, and intense team rivalries.
